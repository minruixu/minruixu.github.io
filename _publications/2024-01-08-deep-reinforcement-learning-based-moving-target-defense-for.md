abstract_text: |-
  The development of LEO satellite networks (LSN) makes them a potential solution to deliver
  broadcast/multicast traffic to deploy and upgrade massive amounts of Internet of Things
  (IoT) devices in future 6G networks. However, inherent resource constraints of LSN leave
  them vulnerable to a multitude of security threats, most notably distributed denial-of-
  service (DDoS) attacks. Existing solutions are primarily based on machine learning detection
  methods which are incapable of defending against unknown zero-day attacks. This paper
  presents an innovative solution leveraging deep reinforcement learning (DRL) to create a
  dynamic multicast tree based on moving target defense (MTD), aimed at enhancing the security
  of multicast services in LSN. The proposed solution adopts an adaptive orbital tree mutation
  (AOTM) scheme that dynamically adjusts multicast tree configurations considering quality of
  service (QoS) constraints to avoid attacks on vulnerable nodes. Simulations demonstrate the
  effectiveness of the AOTM scheme, showcasing its superior defense success rates compared to
  existing state-of-the-art algorithms.
