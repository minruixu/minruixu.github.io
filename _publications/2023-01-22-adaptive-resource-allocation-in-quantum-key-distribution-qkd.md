abstract_text: |-
  Increasing privacy and security concerns in intelligence-native 6G networks require quantum
  key distribution-secured federated learning (QKD-FL), in which data owners connected via
  quantum channels can train an FL global model collaboratively without exposing their local
  datasets. To facilitate QKD-FL, the architectural design and routing management framework
  are essential. However, effective implementation is still lacking. To this end, we propose a
  hierarchical architecture for QKD-FL systems in which QKD resources (i.e., wavelengths) and
  routing are jointly optimized for FL applications. In particular, we focus on adaptive QKD
  resource allocation and routing for FL workers to minimize the deployment cost of QKD nodes
  under various uncertainties, including security requirements. The experimental results show
  that the proposed architecture and the resource allocation and routing model can reduce the
  deployment cost by 7.72\% compared to the CO-QBN algorithm.
